ABOUT ME

I am currently a graduate student at the University of Delaware pursuing a Master of Science in Robotics after recently completing my Bachelors of Science in Mechanical Engineering. I have strong interests in mechanical design, electronics, and machine programming, and look forward to use and develop these skills in a professional setting. My upper level coursework at UDel has been dedicated to control of mobile robots and kinematics of robotic arms, as well as introductory principles surrounding AI algorithms and computer vision.

While at UD, I was heavily involved with the digital fabrication space in our design studio, called the FabLab. Here, I learned plenty of valuable lessons about 3D printing, laser cutting, and electonics, and I developed a strong sense for creating designs with manufacturability in mind. I've also completed plenty of class projects and research projects pertaining towards my interests, which you can find below. These projects helped prepare me for whatever work is in store for me in the future, and they helped grow my passion and confidence in the field.

Senior Design

My senior design project, which I continued past my senior design deadline into my graduate year, with the intention of navigating farming structures to monitor plant health.

Junior Design

A course project, where we created an automated pill bottle production machine using BBs as a stand in for real pills, where we precisely filled and capped the bottles.

Swarm Simulator

A simulated robot swarm and environment to be used by HORC Lab at UD, created using ROS2 Humble and Gazebo Classic.
